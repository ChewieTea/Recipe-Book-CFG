Sweet potato cakes with kimchi mayo

Too bright? Here's Dark-Mode →

Ingredients

For the sweet potato cakes

  • 400g sweet potatoes(2 medium)
  • 1 green finger chilli, very finely chopped
  • 100g sweetcorn, drained
  • a handful of fresh coriander, chopped plus extra to serve
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 fat clove of garlic, crushed
  • 3 spring onions, finely sliced
  • rapeseed oil, to fry

For the kimchi mayo

  • 50g kimchi
  • 100g mayonnaise
  1. Preheat the oven to 180•C fan/200•C/gas 6.
  2. Peel the sweet potatoes and cut into 3cm-thick chunks, then place on an oven tray and bake for 25 to 30 minuntes until tender and a knife slips through them easily. Leave them to cool, then place in a bowl and mash with a fork until smooth.
  3. Meanwhile make the kimchi mayo by blending the kimchi and mayonnaise together, either in the small bowl of a food processor or using a stick blender. Scrape into a serving bowl and leave to one side.
  4. To make the cakes, add the chilli, sweetcorn, coriander, salt, garlic and spring onions to the mashed sweet potato and mix very well with your hands. Bring together into a ball. Cut the ball in half, then cut each half into 4 pieces so you have 8 equal pieces. Take a piece, roll between your palms to form a ball, then flatten into a round patty around 1cm thick. Place on a plate, then shape the rest.
  5. To cook the cakes, heat a teaspoon of oil in a large oil in a large non-stick frying pan over a medium-high heat. When hot, add 4 cakes (or as many as you're able to fit) to the pan and fry for 5 minutes, turning halfway through, or when lovely and golden on each side. (you can help them warm on a plate in the oven at 100•C fan/120•C/250•F/gas1/2.)
  6. When you're ready to eat, place a couple of cakes on each plate, sprinkle with coriander and serve with kimchi mayo.
